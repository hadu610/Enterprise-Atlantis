# How You Get Started · Customer Journeys

> **Type:** Customer-facing guide · **Owner:** Product / Marketing · **Status:** Draft · **Audience:** Founders, COOs, CHROs, CFOs, CTOs evaluating Atlantis · **Last reviewed:** 2026-05-16

Whether you are a five-person startup or a fifty-thousand-person enterprise, you start the same way on Atlantis: **describe your business, get a working AI company in minutes**. What differs is which departments you turn on first, how fast you ramp autonomy, and how deeply we integrate with your existing tools and cloud.

This page shows three paths — **Startup**, **Medium**, and **Enterprise** — so you can see exactly what your first day, first month, and first year look like.

---

## The same first five minutes for everyone

Every customer begins with the **[AI Business Consultant](AI-Business-Consultant-Onboarding)**. You describe your business in plain English; it researches similar companies, pulls the right playbooks from our validated Wiki, and produces a custom blueprint with a ranked department lineup, recommended integrations, and a 30 / 60 / 90 day plan — all cited, all editable, all yours.

You then **pick which departments to activate first**. You do not have to turn everything on. Most customers start with two.

Everyone starts in **Drafting Mode** — agents draft, humans approve. Trust is earned per customer, not assumed. Autonomy expands as evidence accumulates ([Phased Autonomy Model](Phased-Autonomy-Reference)).

## 🚀 The Startup journey *(1–49 people)*

### Why startups choose Atlantis

You do not have the budget or the time to hire a full HR, Finance, Marketing, Sales, Legal, and Ops team. You need leverage — the output of a 50-person company while staying lean. You also do not want to spend your first month wiring up infrastructure.

### Day 1 — *"I described my business and had agents running by lunch"*

1. **Sign up at atlantis.ai.** No procurement, no demo gate.
2. **Five-minute AI Business Consultant session.** Describe what you do, who you sell to, where you operate, what hurts most.
3. **Custom blueprint produced in minutes** — ranked departments, recommended integrations, jurisdiction-aware compliance starting point.
4. **Pick 1–2 departments.** Revenue-stage startups usually start with **Sales + Marketing**. Engineering-led startups start with **Dev**. Funded startups often add **Finance** for bookkeeping and runway tracking.
5. **Connect via OAuth** — HubSpot or Salesforce, Slack, Stripe, GitHub, Gmail. Add others when you need them.
6. **Agents start drafting** — emails, posts, briefs, code, financial reports. You approve before anything goes out.

### Week 1–4 — building trust

- Agents work in **Drafting Mode**. You review every output. The Trust Score Dashboard shows accuracy and your override rate.
- The platform learns your voice, your offers, your customers' objections, your engineering conventions — and writes them into your private Wiki, which only your team and your agents can read.
- You move to **Startup Mode** for low-risk read actions (lead enrichment, CRM hygiene, code review comments). Writes still require your sign-off.

### Month 2–6 — leverage

- Add a third department when you need it (HR when you hit your first hire; Legal when you sign your first enterprise contract).
- Agents move to **Approval Mode** for proven workflow categories. Sales agent qualifies inbound and schedules meetings autonomously; Marketing agent publishes campaign emails after a single human approval; Dev agent ships small PRs to production with validation gates.
- Headcount stays flat as revenue grows. You hire **specialists for judgment**, not for execution.

### Year 1 outcome

A 12-person company that operates like a 60-person company. Lead-to-customer cycles cut by 40%. Sales pipeline grew 3×. Founders stop being the bottleneck on every campaign, every contract, every PR review.

### What you pay

**Starter tier — from $30K / year.** Up to two department agents, five Data Bridge connectors, self-serve Change Management, shared CSM pool. Upgrade to Growth when you outgrow it (most customers do, around 50 employees).

## 🏢 The Medium-business journey *(50–249 people)*

### Why medium-size companies choose Atlantis

You have multiple departments, several specialist hires, and growing operational complexity. You are scaling — and you do not want to scale headcount linearly with revenue. You want **AI-augmented departments**, not point tools that produce more dashboards.

### Week 1 — guided onboarding

1. **Half-day kickoff** with a named Customer Success Manager (CSM). Your founder or COO walks through current org chart, top three pain points, top compliance constraints, and existing tool stack.
2. **AI Business Consultant session** with broader inputs — multiple operating jurisdictions, more complex revenue model, existing team structures to preserve.
3. **Custom blueprint produced** — usually 4 departments to start. Common opening set: **Sales + Marketing + HR + Finance**. Operations and Legal added in months 2–6.
4. **Integration plan** — Salesforce or HubSpot, Workday or BambooHR, NetSuite or QuickBooks, Slack or Teams, Jira, GitHub, Snowflake. The CSM owns connector activation; nothing in your stack is "future roadmap."

### Month 1 — pilot one department deeply

- One department (typically HR or Marketing) goes to **Drafting + Startup Mode** first. The CSM and your champion review weekly. The Trust Score Dashboard accumulates evidence.
- The Universal Data Bridge normalises data across your tools — employee record from Workday + Slack + Okta becomes a single canonical entity. Agents read the canonical form, not five disconnected APIs.
- Change Management Module rolls out internally: employees see what each agent does and does not do; managers see who approves what.

### Quarter 1–2 — agents in production

- Three to four departments running. Two are in **Approval Mode** for proven workflow categories. The Trust Score Dashboard is reviewed in your quarterly business review.
- Cross-agent coordination via the [Action Executor](Cross-Agent-Coordination) means HR's record update and Finance's payroll change can never corrupt the same employee record.
- You begin **net-new workflows** that were never possible before: real-time campaign attribution that updates Salesforce + HubSpot + Slack simultaneously; weekly close that drafts itself by Friday.

### Year 1 outcome

You held headcount flat in three departments while revenue grew 60%. Two of your departments are running in Approval Mode; the others are in Startup Mode and ramping. Your CFO has cycle-time evidence to defend the platform fee 8× over.

### What you pay

**Growth tier — from $90K / year.** Four agents included, 15 Data Bridge sources, named CSM, install certified marketplace playbooks. Optional add-ons: BYOK ($25K/yr), dedicated cell ($100K/yr), additional agent ($18K/yr).

## 🏛️ The Enterprise journey *(250+ people)*

### Why enterprises choose Atlantis

You have data spread across Salesforce, ServiceNow, Workday, SAP, Snowflake, M365 — and a CISO who will not accept another SaaS island. You operate across multiple jurisdictions, with regulatory exposure in several of them. You have **failed** AI agent pilots before. You need a platform that solves the [six structural barriers](The-Six-Barriers), not another assistant.

### Phase 0 — security and procurement *(2–6 weeks)*

- CISO review: SOC 2 Type II report, DPA, sub-processor list, encryption posture, agent identity architecture, [Validation Gate Specifications](Validation-Gate-Specifications), audit log model.
- Procurement: MSA, DPA, BAA if applicable, custom AI clauses (model substitution, prompt evolution policy, audit access).
- Architecture review: managed cell vs **bring-your-own-cloud** in your AWS / GCP / Azure account. Most enterprise customers start in a managed cell and graduate to BYOC when central IT pulls them in.

### Phase 1 — land in one business unit *(month 1–3)*

- One business unit (typically a P&L owner with acute pain) goes through the **AI Business Consultant** — same five-minute experience, with the CSM and TAM facilitating.
- The Consultant pulls **your enterprise blueprints** (sales playbook, brand guidelines, compliance policies) into the customer's private Wiki, alongside our validated industry playbooks.
- One department, in Drafting Mode. The Trust Score Dashboard is shared with the CIO and CISO every two weeks.
- Domain Expert Council reviews any output that touches regulated subject matter (jurisdiction-specific employment law, GAAP/IFRS, healthcare PII, financial regulations).

### Phase 2 — expand across departments *(months 4–12)*

- Add departments to the same business unit. Cross-agent coordination through the [Action Executor](Cross-Agent-Coordination) handles every shared entity (employee, customer, account, deal, ticket, transaction) under entity-keyed serialisation with optimistic concurrency control.
- Validation Gates run silently between agent handoffs. The Rollback Engine takes care of any action that breaches policy.
- The first department reaches **Approval Mode**; trust evidence is now public inside your org.

### Phase 3 — expand across business units *(year 2)*

- Replicate the proven configuration to additional units. Each unit is a **tenant within your enterprise account** — isolated data, isolated wiki, isolated audit log, but shared policies and shared playbooks where you choose to share them.
- BYOC switched on if central IT requires it: the agent runtime executes inside your AWS / GCP / Azure under your IAM and your encryption keys. Atlantis manages the control plane; you own the data plane.
- Some workflows reach **Enterprise Mode** — policy-based auto-approval for pre-approved categories, with human review at exceptions only. Delete and financial actions always require a human (hard rule).

### Year 2 outcome

Five-plus departments running across multiple business units. Two departments are in Enterprise Mode for narrow, well-evidenced workflow categories. The Trust Score Dashboard is the canonical view of agent performance, reviewed at the executive level. Headcount across affected departments held flat through a year of growth; capacity in those departments doubled.

### What you pay

**Enterprise tier — from $250K / year**, or **Enterprise Regulated — from $400K / year** for healthcare, financial services, defence, and other regulated industries. All seven department agents + Dev Agent included; unlimited Data Bridge connectors; named CSM + TAM + GC contact; dedicated cell available; self-hosted / sovereign LLMs available in Regulated tier; 99.95–99.99% SLA.

## Multi-tenancy: what it means for you

Atlantis runs many companies on the same platform. **Multi-tenancy is built in from the first line of code — there is no "single-tenant mode" we will evolve into later.** ([Architecture Principle 9](Architecture-Principles#9-multi-tenancy-first).)

For you, this means:

| Concern | What multi-tenancy guarantees |
|---|---|
| **Your data is yours** | Every database query carries your `tenant_id`. Cache keys are tenant-prefixed. Audit log entries carry your tenant identity. There is no surface where one customer can read another customer's data. |
| **Your wiki is yours** | The playbooks, rules, and company context the AI Business Consultant generates for you live in your private Wiki. Other customers do not see them. You can choose to install **shared** playbooks from the certified marketplace, but the boundary is explicit. |
| **Your agents have your identity** | Each agent identity is scoped to your tenant, your OAuth grants, your data residency choice. An agent cannot reach another customer's systems. |
| **Your audit log is yours** | Every action, every approval, every rollback is logged under your tenant. Audit retention follows your tier (1 / 3 / 7+ years). You can export the full trail. |
| **Noisy-neighbour protection** | At scale, customers are partitioned into [cells](Architecture-Principles#13-cell-based-architecture-for-tenant-isolation) — independent infrastructure stacks. A fault in one cell cannot affect customers in another. Enterprise tier customers can opt for a dedicated cell. |
| **Your billing is yours** | Tier inclusions, usage quotas, and overages are tracked per tenant. No bundled billing across customers. |
| **Exit and portability** | You can export your wiki, audit log, and agent configurations at any time. BYOC customers run the data plane in their own cloud — there is no exit project, just a hand-off. |

**For enterprises with multiple business units:** each unit can be a sub-tenant inside your enterprise account, with isolated data and shared policies where you choose to share them. One billing relationship, many isolated workspaces.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I need a cloud account?**
No. Our managed cloud is the default. **[Bring-your-own-cloud](Architecture-Principles#16-managed-first-with-customer-owned-integrations-and-bring-your-own-cloud-as-an-option)** is available as an Enterprise option if your CISO or compliance scope requires it.

**Can I start with one department and add others later?**
Yes — that is the recommended path. Most customers start with one or two. Adding a department is one click and inherits the blueprint, integrations, and policies already configured.

**Will my data be used to train your models?**
No. Your data is yours; it does not flow into shared model training. We use foundation models from named providers under their commercial APIs.

**What if my industry is not in your playbook library?**
The AI Business Consultant tells you honestly when we do not have a domain playbook for your industry. We can still configure agents on top of the universal patterns (sales, finance, HR), and we co-build the domain layer with you. Honesty over fake coverage.

**Can I see who approved what?**
Yes. Every action — every draft, every approval, every rollback, every integration call — is logged in your tenant's audit log under the responsible identity (human or agent).

**Can I roll back an agent action?**
Yes. The [Rollback Engine](Rollback-Procedures) is built into the platform. Every Write action has a defined inverse operation.

**What happens if an agent makes a mistake?**
In Drafting Mode, mistakes do not reach production — you approve before anything ships. In higher autonomy phases, [Validation Gates](Validation-Gate-Specifications) catch known failure modes before action; the Rollback Engine reverses anything that escapes them; the [Incident Response Playbook](Incident-Response-Playbook) governs the postmortem.

**How do I know it is working?**
The Trust Score Dashboard shows agent accuracy, override rate, value delivered, and the cycle-time reduction per workflow. Your CSM reviews it with you on a cadence appropriate to your tier.
